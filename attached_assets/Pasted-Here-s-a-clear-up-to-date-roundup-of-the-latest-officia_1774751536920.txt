🏛️ Latest Official Updates on Suffolk Devolution & Local Government Reorganisation
📌 Government Decision (Official – GOV.UK)
The Secretary of State has formally decided that Suffolk will move to three new unitary councils, replacing the current county + district/borough structure.

Key points:

Decision issued 25 March 2026.

Three new unitaries will be created.

This follows the Government’s assessment of proposals submitted by Suffolk councils.

📌 Suffolk County Council Response (Official – SCC Website)
Suffolk County Council strongly opposes the Government’s decision, arguing that a single unitary authority would have been more financially resilient and better for service delivery.

SCC’s main concerns:

Splitting Suffolk into three councils risks service disruption, especially for vulnerable residents.

SCC claims the three‑unitary model will leave Suffolk £145m worse off in the first 5 years.

They argue the Government ignored evidence supporting a single county‑wide authority.

📌 District & Borough Councils’ Joint Statement (Official – Ipswich Borough Council)
All district and borough councils (Babergh, East Suffolk, Ipswich, Mid Suffolk, West Suffolk) welcome the Government’s decision and support the three‑unitary model.

Their position:

They believe three councils will be “large enough to deliver, local enough to care.”

Elections for shadow authorities will take place next year.

New councils will formally begin in April 2028.

They argue this structure better reflects Suffolk’s diverse communities.
